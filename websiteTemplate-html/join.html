Join the Club

Become part of our innovative robotics community at St. Clair College

Why Join?

Build Your Network

Connect with like-minded students, industry professionals, and mentors passionate about robotics and engineering.

Gain Practical Skills

Learn CAD, PCB design, programming, and fabrication through real projects - skills highly valued by employers.

Flexible Commitment

Participate as much as you want - from weekly meetings to intensive project work. No prior experience required!

How to Join

1

Join Our Discord

Connect with us on Discord where we coordinate meetings, share updates, and chat about robotics.

2

Attend a Meeting

Come to one of our regular club meetings to meet the team, see what we're working on, and get a feel for the club. Meeting times are posted on Discord.

3

Get Involved

Jump into a project that interests you! Whether it's mechanical design, electronics, or software, there's a place for everyone. We'll help you get started.

What to Expect

Weekly Meetings

Regular club meetings for project updates, skill-sharing workshops, and planning sessions.

Project Work

Hands-on time in the lab building, testing, and iterating on robotics projects.

Workshops

Learn new skills through member-led workshops on CAD, programming, electronics, and more.

Social Events

Team building activities, demo days, and celebrations of our accomplishments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior robotics experience?

Not at all! We welcome students of all skill levels. Whether you're a complete beginner or have years of experience, there's a place for you in the club.

Is there a membership fee?

Membership is currently free for all St. Clair College students. Materials and tools for projects are provided by the club.

How much time do I need to commit?

As much or as little as you want! Some members come to every meeting, while others drop in when they can. We understand that students have busy schedules.

What programs/majors are members from?

Our members come from various programs including Engineering, Computer Science, Technology, and more. Diversity in backgrounds makes our projects better!

Still Have Questions?

Feel free to reach out to us! We're happy to answer any questions you have about joining the club.
